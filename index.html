About Me.

Hi! I'm Omar, and I'm going to change the world for the better.

  • I grew up in Cupertino, California, where I was blessed to get a head-start with technology. I went to my first hackathon as a high school junior, and I immediately got hooked on tech.

  • For college, I came to Carnegie Mellon University for Electrical and Computer Engineering, with a minor in Design.

  • After my first year at CMU, I contributed to FreeCodeCamp , an open source curriculum designed to teach anyone how to code.

  • Over the summer of 2017, I interned at Nutanix where I learned a ton and had a blast!

  • Currently, I'm wrapping up the fall semester with a plan to create two projects every week over break. Keep up with my progress on the Projects page!

If you didnt know...

MellonHeads is an organization on CMU's campus directed towards making students familiar with hackathons. Hackathons are a valuable, albeit tiring, experience that cultivate some amazing projects. See some examples of our meetings below!

Hack to School

At our biannual "Hack to School" event, we introduced the concept of hackathons to many newcomers. Interestingly enough, with CMU's insane reputation in the tech industry, one would think that hackathons would be very popular. However, it's just the contrary! We explained what a hackathon was and how to brainstorm for it, and the event was a smash hit! Attached is a video recap of the event.
